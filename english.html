English.

English is a West Germanic language that originated in England. Its history can be traced back to the 5th and 6th centuries when Germanic tribes, specifically the Angles, Saxons, and Jutes, migrated to Britain. Over time, Old English evolved and absorbed influences from Norse and Norman French. Middle English emerged after the Norman Conquest of 1066, and Modern English began taking shape around the late 15th century. Today, English has become a global language, spoken by millions worldwide and serving as a lingua franca in various fields.
