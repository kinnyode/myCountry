Russian.

Russian, an East Slavic language, traces its roots to the Old East Slavic language spoken in Kievan Rus' during the medieval period. Over time, it evolved through the influence of other Slavic languages and foreign influences such as Mongolian and Tatar. The standardization of the Russian language occurred during the 18th and 19th centuries. Today, Russian is spoken by approximately 170 million native speakers, primarily in Russia and other countries of the former Soviet Union.
