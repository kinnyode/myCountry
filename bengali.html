Bengali.

Bengali, an Indo-Aryan language, evolved from the Magadhi Prakrit spoken in the Bengal region of the Indian subcontinent. It developed as a distinct language during the medieval period. The 19th century Bengal Renaissance played a significant role in the modernization and standardization of Bengali. Today, Bengali is the official language of Bangladesh and one of the 22 scheduled languages of India, with approximately 230 million native speakers.
