Spanish.

Spanish, a Romance language, originated on the Iberian Peninsula. Its roots can be traced back to the Vulgar Latin spoken by the Roman settlers in the region. With the expansion of the Kingdom of Castile in the 13th century, Spanish began to develop as a distinct language. Through colonization, Spanish spread to the Americas, Africa, and Asia, becoming one of the most widely spoken languages globally. Today, it serves as the official language of 21 countries and has over 460 million native speakers.
