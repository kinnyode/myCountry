Fried Rice.

Fried rice originated in China during the Sui Dynasty (589-618 AD) as a way to use leftover rice. It was initially a simple dish of stir-frying rice with soy sauce and various ingredients like vegetables, meat, and seafood. Over time, fried rice became popular throughout Asia, with different regional variations and flavor profiles. Today, it is enjoyed worldwide as a versatile and satisfying dish.
