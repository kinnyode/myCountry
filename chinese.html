Chinese.

Chinese, a Sino-Tibetan language, has a long history dating back over 3,000 years. Its origins can be traced to the Shang Dynasty during the 2nd millennium BCE. Classical Chinese, known as Literary Chinese, was used for formal writing and communication. Modern Standard Chinese, based on the Mandarin dialect, became the official language of China in the 20th century. With over 1.2 billion native speakers, Chinese is one of the most widely spoken languages in the world, representing a rich cultural heritage and diverse regional dialects.
