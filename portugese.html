Portugese.

Portuguese, a Romance language, traces its origins to Latin, which was brought to the Iberian Peninsula by the Romans. With the establishment of the Kingdom of Portugal in the 12th century, Portuguese emerged as a distinct language. Portugal's maritime exploration in the 15th and 16th centuries spread the language to regions such as Brazil, Africa, and Asia. Today, Portuguese is spoken by approximately 220 million native speakers, making it one of the major global languages.
