Hindi.

Hindi, an Indo-Aryan language, evolved from the ancient language Sanskrit. It emerged as a distinct language around the 7th century CE in North India. Influenced by Persian, Arabic, and other regional languages, Hindi developed further during the medieval period. With India's independence in 1947, Hindi became one of the official languages of the country. Today, Hindi is widely spoken by approximately 340 million native speakers primarily in India and is an integral part of the vibrant Indian culture.
