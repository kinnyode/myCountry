The Colosseum, Rome, Italy.

The Colosseum, located in Rome, Italy, is an ancient amphitheater built during the Flavian dynasty in 70-80 AD. It was primarily used for gladiatorial contests and public spectacles, accommodating up to 50,000 spectators. The iconic structure has suffered damage from earthquakes and looting over the centuries but remains a symbol of Roman engineering and entertainment. Today, it stands as one of Rome's most famous landmarks and a testament to the grandeur of ancient Roman architecture.
