Arabic.

Arabic, a Semitic language, has a history dating back to the 4th century CE. It evolved from the Proto-Arabic language spoken in the Arabian Peninsula. With the rise of Islam in the 7th century, Arabic became the language of the Quran and spread across the Islamic empire. It played a crucial role in the development of science, literature, and trade during the Islamic Golden Age. Today, Arabic is one of the most widely spoken languages, with over 315 million native speakers primarily in the Middle East and North Africa.
