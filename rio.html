CCR, Rio De Janero.

Corcovado and Cristo Redentor (Christ the Redeemer) are iconic landmarks in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. The statue of Christ the Redeemer, located atop Corcovado Mountain, was completed in 1931 and stands at 98 feet (30 meters) tall. It is a symbol of Christianity and a popular tourist attraction. The monument was designed by Paul Landowski and built in collaboration with Heitor da Silva Costa and Albert Caquot. Today, it is recognized as one of the New Seven Wonders of the World and offers stunning panoramic views of Rio de Janeiro.
